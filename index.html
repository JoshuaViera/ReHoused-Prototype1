Know your rights
Tap any topic to expand. All information is for guidance only — consult a housing attorney or Legal Aid for specific legal advice.

▶ Right to shelter

New York City is legally required to provide shelter to anyone who needs it, under the Callahan v. Carey consent decree (1981). You cannot be turned away. If you are denied shelter, call 311 immediately.

▶ Source of income discrimination

It is illegal for landlords or brokers to refuse to rent to you because you have a CityFHEPS voucher. This is called source of income discrimination. Report violations to the NYC Commission on Human Rights at 212-416-0197 or HRA's SOI team at 929-221-6576 / soi@dss.nyc.gov.

▶ CityFHEPS late fee protections

A landlord cannot evict you for late fees on the CityFHEPS portion of your rent. As long as DSS's monthly payment is made within the month it's due, it is not considered late. Landlords also cannot take you to small claims court for late fees on the CityFHEPS portion.

▶ Cash Assistance fair hearing rights

If your Cash Assistance is denied or terminated, you have the right to a fair hearing. You can request one and continue receiving benefits during the appeal process. Call the HRA Infoline at 718-557-1399 for assistance.

▶ Right to legal counsel in housing court

Under NYC's Right to Counsel law, eligible tenants facing eviction in housing court have the right to free legal representation. Contact the Legal Aid Society or call 311 to find your local provider.

▶ Shelter conditions & personal property

You have the right to safe and clean shelter conditions, personal property storage, mail and phone access, and regular visiting hours. To file a complaint about shelter conditions, call 311 or contact the DHS complaint line.

▶ How to file complaints

NYC Commission on Human Rights: 212-416-0197. DHS complaints: call 311. HRA Infoline: 718-557-1399. HRA Source of Income team: 929-221-6576. Legal Aid Society: 212-577-3300.
